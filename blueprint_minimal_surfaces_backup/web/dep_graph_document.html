Definition 12Almost minimizing

A surface \(\Sigma \) is \(\varepsilon \)-a.m. in \(U\) if there is no isotopy supported in \(U\) which keeps area within \(\varepsilon /8\) along the path and decreases the final area by at least \(\varepsilon \).

Definition 2Continuous Family

A family \(\{ \Sigma _t\} _{t \in [0,1]}\) of surfaces of \(M\) is said to be continuous if:

  • \(\mathcal{H}^2(\Sigma _t)\) is a continuous function of \(t\);

  • \(\Sigma _t \to \Sigma _{t_0}\) in the Hausdorff topology whenever \(t \to t_0\).

Definition 3Generalized Family of Surfaces

A family \(\{ \Sigma _t\} _{t \in [0,1]}\) of subsets of \(M\) is said to be a generalized family of surfaces if there are a finite subset \(T\) of \([0, 1]\) and a finite set of points \(P\) in \(M\) such that:

  • Conditions (c1) and (c2) from Definition 2 hold;

  • \(\Sigma _t\) is a surface for every \(t \notin T\);

  • For \(t \in T\), \(\Sigma _t\) is a surface in \(M \setminus P\).

Definition 4Generalized family

A family \(\{ \Sigma _t\} _{t\in [0,1]}\) with Hausdorff-continuous area and Hausdorff convergence in \(t\), allowing finitely many parameter values with point singularities.

Lemma 13Convergence regularity

If a min–max sequence converges in the varifold sense, its limit contains a smooth embedded minimal surface.

Theorem 1Simon–Smith, Colding–De Lellis

For any saturated set of generalized families of surfaces \(\Lambda \), there exists a min–max sequence obtained from \(\Lambda \) converging in the varifold sense to a smooth embedded minimal surface of area \(m_0(\Lambda )\) (multiplicity allowed).

Theorem 14Simon–Smith

Let \(M\) be a closed Riemannian \(3\)-manifold. For any saturated set \(\Lambda \) of generalized families of surfaces, there exists a min–max sequence obtained from \(\Lambda \) and converging in the varifold sense to a smooth embedded minimal surface with area \(m_0(\Lambda )\) (multiplicity allowed).
