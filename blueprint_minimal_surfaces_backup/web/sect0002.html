Theorem 1 Simon–Smith, Colding–De Lellis

For any saturated set of generalized families of surfaces \(\Lambda \), there exists a min–max sequence obtained from \(\Lambda \) converging in the varifold sense to a smooth embedded minimal surface of area \(m_0(\Lambda )\) (multiplicity allowed).

This theorem represents the culmination of the min–max theory for minimal surfaces.

Definition 2 Continuous Family

  • MinimalSurfaces.ContinuousFamily

A family \(\{ \Sigma _t\} _{t \in [0,1]}\) of surfaces of \(M\) is said to be continuous if:

  • \(\mathcal{H}^2(\Sigma _t)\) is a continuous function of \(t\);

  • \(\Sigma _t \to \Sigma _{t_0}\) in the Hausdorff topology whenever \(t \to t_0\).

  • MinimalSurfaces.GeneralizedFamilyOfSurfaces

A family \(\{ \Sigma _t\} _{t \in [0,1]}\) of subsets of \(M\) is said to be a generalized family of surfaces if there are a finite subset \(T\) of \([0, 1]\) and a finite set of points \(P\) in \(M\) such that:

  • Conditions (c1) and (c2) from Definition 2 hold;

  • \(\Sigma _t\) is a surface for every \(t \notin T\);

  • For \(t \in T\), \(\Sigma _t\) is a surface in \(M \setminus P\).
